  SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle) deals, while noting that institutional investors have
  zero interest in non-AI deals currently. However, he emphasizes that regardless

  competitive and find the intersection of AI capabilities with deep domain expertise.


  Gurley shares insights from his recent 10-day trip to China, challenging common
  American misconceptions about the country. He explains that China operates more
  through competitive provincial systems than top-down communist control, leading
  to intense innovation and remarkable execution in industries like solar, EVs, and
  robotics. He toured Xiaomi''s car factory and was impressed by their systematic
  approach to product development and manufacturing efficiency. Gurley argues that
  American policy discussions about China are often based on misunderstandings of
  what''s actually happening there, and warns that the U.S. faces significant challenges
  in manufacturing cost competitiveness and infrastructure development speed.


  The conversation transitions to Gurley''s book, which provides a framework for pursuing
  non-traditional career paths. Key principles include: going where the action is
  (physical proximity to industry centers still matters despite digital tools), embracing
  peers for shared learning, and ensuring genuine passion through the "free time learning"
  test - if you wouldn''t study something in your spare time, you''re probably not
  truly passionate about it. Gurley shares compelling stories from figures like Jerry
  Seinfeld, Bob Dylan, and restaurateur Danny Meyer, all of whom gained deep foundational
  knowledge before innovating. He concludes by discussing his next chapter: potentially
